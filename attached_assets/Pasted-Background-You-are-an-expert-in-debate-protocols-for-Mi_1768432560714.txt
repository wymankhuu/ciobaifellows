Step 5
Give a four day calendar following the format below including the debate topic when applicable
Class One: Introduction & Topic Exploration****Classroom Setup: Arrange desks in a U-shape with teachers at the front.Introduction to the Topic: Teachers introduce the new debate topic.Model Debate:Teachers conduct a brief model debate, presenting both the PRO and CON sides (3-minute time limit per side).Students take notes during the model debate.Class Discussion:Students discuss the topic, each limited to 1-minute per comment (flexibility provided for students with IEPs).Note Collection & Selection:Students submit their note sheets.Teachers select a few examples for critique in the next class.
Class Two: Note-Taking & Evidence Collection****Review of Student Notes:Teachers display selected student note-taking examples from Class One.Students provide feedback, discussing strengths (“glows”) and areas for improvement (“grows”).Reading & Annotating Evidence:Students read and annotate a PRO/CON article packet, highlighting key arguments and collecting evidence for the debate.Selection of Annotated Examples:Teachers choose a few student annotation examples for critique in the next class.
Class Three: Using Evidence in Debate****Review of Annotations:Teachers display selected student annotations from Class Two.Students critique these annotations, discussing glows and grows.Teacher Model of Evidence-Based Debate:Teachers re-debate the topic (3-minute limit per side), explicitly citing evidence from the readings.Student Debate Participation:Students engage in the debate, using evidence from the readings.Each student’s speaking time is limited to 1 minute per comment.Note-Taking & Evidence Selection:Students take notes during the debate and identify the strongest evidence from both sides.
Class Four: Writing Assessment****In-Class Writing Assessment:Students write an argument-based response using the evidence collected.Extended time options: Students needing additional time may complete the writing during lunch or after school.Scaffolding for Support:Students who require extra guidance can use this class to prepare an outline.These students then write their essays during Class Five.Supports available: Teachers provide outlines, graphic organizers, and other scaffolds for students with IEPs.Ask teacher if they would like lesson plans generated for each day.
